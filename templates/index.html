Washington’s Climate Policy: What Needs to Happen

Washington law requires deep greenhouse gas reductions: 45% by 2030, 70% by 2040, and 95% by 2050. Transportation is the state’s single largest emissions source, so adoption of clean vehicles is central to reaching these legally mandated milestones.

The state has adopted California’s Advanced Clean Cars and Advanced Clean Cars II standards, gradually increasing the required share of zero-emission new vehicle sales. Your tools model how charger build-out and EV adoption trajectories move counties — and the state — toward these targets.

Tesla Market Share: Strategic Implications

Tesla still commands roughly half of U.S. EV sales, but faces steady competitive pressure. Fast-charging access — especially along I-5, I-90, and urban hubs — remains one of Tesla’s strongest growth levers.

As Washington increases its clean-vehicle requirements, annual EV demand rises sharply. Your scenarios help reveal:

  • • Where added superchargers unlock new Tesla demand
  • • Which counties offer the highest incremental EV growth
  • • How statewide EV adoption shifts Tesla’s potential market share
  • • Where competitors may gain first-mover advantage

The result: a dual-purpose tool for both public-sector climate planning and private-sector EV strategy.
